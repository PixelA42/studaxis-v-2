Class 5 EVS: Seeds and Germination (Sample Notes)
Topic: How a Seed Becomes a Plant
Subject: Environmental Studies (EVS)
Grade: 5
1. What is a Seed?
A seed is a tiny life form that contains the food and instructions needed to grow into a new plant. It has a protective outer layer called the Seed Coat.
2. Conditions for Germination
For a seed to wake up and start growing, it needs three essential things:
Air: To breathe.
Water: To soften the seed coat and start chemical reactions.
Warmth: To provide the energy for growth (Sunlight).
3. Parts of a Seed
Seed Coat: The hard outer covering.
Cotyledons: The "seed leaves" that store food for the baby plant.
Embryo: The baby plant inside the seed.
4. The Process of Germination
The seed absorbs water and swells up.
The Radicle (baby root) breaks through the seed coat and grows downward.
The Plumule (baby shoot) grows upward toward the light.
Once leaves appear, the plant starts making its own food through Photosynthesis.
5. Fun Fact: Seeds that Travel
Seeds don't just stay in one place! They move via:
Wind: Light seeds like Dandelions.
Water: Floating seeds like Coconuts.
Animals: Sticky seeds (Xanthium) or seeds eaten and dropped by birds.
Experimental Task:
Soak a few Moong or Chana seeds in a wet cloth for 2 days. Observe how the small white "tail" (the root) starts to emerge!
